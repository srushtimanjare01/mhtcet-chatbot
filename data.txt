MHT-CET stands for Maharashtra Common Entrance Test.

The MHT-CET exam includes three subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

The syllabus is based on Class 11 and Class 12 Maharashtra State Board.

Top engineering colleges include COEP Pune, VJTI Mumbai, and SPIT Mumbai.

COEP Pune cutoff is usually around 99 percentile.

VJTI Mumbai cutoff is around 98-99 percentile.

A good percentile in MHT-CET is above 90.

Preparation tips:
- Study NCERT and Maharashtra board books
- Practice previous year papers
- Focus on formulas and concepts

The exam is conducted by Maharashtra State CET Cell.

MHT-CET is usually held once a year.

There is no negative marking in MHT-CET.

Mathematics has more weightage compared to Physics and Chemistry.
